Entry Activity

Convergence!

The goal: converge on a single word — starting from two random, unrelated words that describe the experience you just had.

  • Two volunteers count down from 3, then each say one word.
  • The first two people who think of a word related to both say “Got it!” — then say their words together after a 3-count.
  • Repeat until two people say the same word. The group has converged!

What Gets in the Way

Conditions That Erode Belonging

No Specific Noticing

People are present, but not personally seen.

Performative Belonging

The space says “you belong,” but doesn’t feel that way.

Low Agency or Voice

People participate, but don’t shape the experience.

Hidden Rules

Expectations are unclear or assumed.

Availability without Initiation

Help exists, but only if people ask.

Conditional Mattering

People feel valued only when they comply or perform.

Uneven Attention

Attention goes to the most visible people.

Heat Map · Instructions

Map Where Belonging Lives

Prompt: Where do we hypothesize students experience belonging — and what evidence would we need to confirm it?

  • As a team, analyze your site’s belonging heat map using two colored markers.
  • Color 1 — highlight patterns where belonging appears strong.
  • Color 2 — highlight patterns where belonging appears weak or inconsistent.

Heat Map · Analysis

Step Back & Reflect

Look at both colors on the map together, then ask:

  • Where do you see consistent strengths?
  • Where do you see consistent gaps?
  • Which patterns connect to adult culture, classroom practice, or both?
  • What would it take to make the strengths the norm — not the exception?

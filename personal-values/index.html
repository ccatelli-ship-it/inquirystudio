Personal Values Activity

Help teachers identify the core values that shape their decisions, relationships, and design choices.

Stats

Suggested Time
20–35 minutes
Level
Easy
Materials
Values list; pen; reflection space
Participants
Individuals first, then teams
The Arc
ScanNarrowShare

The Values Activity helps participants move from a broad list of meaningful values to a short list of core values. For teacher teams, the activity creates language for what matters most, where values align, and where values may create productive tension in collaborative work.

Open the Values List (PDF)

Steps

01

Scan the list. Read through the values list and add any values that feel important but are missing.

02

Choose ten. Circle ten values that feel most important to you right now in your work and life.

03

Narrow to five. Cross off five of the ten. Notice which values are hard to let go of and why.

04

Narrow to three. Cross off two more, leaving the three values that feel most core.

05

Reflect individually. Write a quick note: How do these values show up in my actions? Where is there alignment? Where might I need to recalibrate?

06

Share as a team. Invite each person to share their three values. Listen for patterns, differences, and values that should guide the team's next design or planning decision.
